Our Story
How We Met
We met in a student organization at Cal State Long Beach, HSBA. Instantly, we became friends. We would carpool to activities and events since we lived a city away.
Our First Date
Our first date was on Valentine's Day. After our original plans fell through, we decided as friends to spend the evening at the Block of Orange while grabbing a bite to eat at Johnny Rockets and catching a Kevin Hart film at AMC. Who knew this last minute date would've been the spark to something special.
Our Favorite Activities
We LOVE to watch and attend Dodger games! We also enjoy going to Dave & Busters to play a few games, catch a movie with our AMC passes, or wait in lines at Disneyland. Our newest hobbies include cooking new recipes in our home and watching the Bachelor!
How Johnny Popped The Question
I coordinated a Mothers Day brunch in Laguna Beach at Las Brisas. I had to work with Christina’s family to make sure she got her nails done, take a ring from her jewelry box to get the correct size, all while keeping the proposal a secret! I waited for her alongside a cliff with a gold rose in my hand, rose petals on the floor, overlooking the ocean. Christina came walking down the path asking herself “What are you doing Johnny!?”
Before getting on one knee, I had her witness all of our family and random bystanders watching our moment. I then pulled out an image of her grandparents and my grandmother who had passed, to symbolize their presence as I asked for her to marry me.
Our Civil Wedding
With the COVID restrictions, we decided to keep our original date September 6th. We hosted a zoom call for all of our family and friends to join our civil union. We wanted someone special in our relationship to lead our ceremony all while having our parents, siblings and grandparents present. We asked Christina’s nina, Olivia, to officiate our marriage and our siblings, Arlene and Mikey, to certify our marriage license.
